## Coalition shifts

### `intv_training` -> `intv_drug_discovery`
_Invest in AI workforce training and retraining programs. -> Deploy frontier AI (structure prediction, candidate screening, trial simulation) inside drug discovery and therapeutic development pipelines targeting neglected infectious disease, antimicrobial resistance, and LMIC-priority therapeutics._

**Gaining:** **Global health & effective giving**, **Biomedical research**, **Anthropic**
**Losing:** **Displaced workers**

Training-side friction was labor-facing: displacement pacing, reskilling throughput, political backlash from the employee layer. Drug discovery shifts the friction to wet-lab validation, FDA pathway, and IP/pricing capture --- a totally different bind. Biomedical and global-health camps pick up the slack on legitimacy, which makes the move easier to sell in Congress and harder to sell to labor. The -0.033 harm delta is real but small; it reflects dual-use bio concerns at the destination, not a scoring artifact.

### `intv_grid` -> `intv_drug_discovery`
_Accelerate grid and generation buildout (permitting reform, interconnection, new generation). -> Deploy frontier AI (structure prediction, candidate screening, trial simulation) inside drug discovery and therapeutic development pipelines targeting neglected infectious disease, antimicrobial resistance, and LMIC-priority therapeutics._

**Gaining:** **Global health & effective giving**, **Biomedical research**, **Animal welfare**
**Losing:** **Palantir**

Grid is capex-bound and regulator-bound at the substrate layer --- PJM interconnect queues, NEPA, utility commissions. Drug discovery binds on trial capacity, regulatory approval, and compute access. The +0.267 harm robustness gain is genuine: grid buildout carries siting, emissions, and concentration harms that drug discovery doesn't replicate. Palantir-style national-advantage framing weakens because pharma R&D is less legibly a strategic asset than power infrastructure. Not an artifact.

### `intv_alt_protein` -> `intv_drug_discovery`
_Accelerate alternative-protein development (precision fermentation, cultivated meat, plant-based) with AI-driven strain engineering, scaffolding optimization, and supply-chain cost-down, targeting displacement of factory-farm protein at scale. -> Deploy frontier AI (structure prediction, candidate screening, trial simulation) inside drug discovery and therapeutic development pipelines targeting neglected infectious disease, antimicrobial resistance, and LMIC-priority therapeutics._

**Gaining:** **Global health & effective giving**, **Biomedical research**
**Losing:** **Animal welfare**

Alt protein friction is consumer-acceptance and unit-economics; drug discovery friction is regulatory and capital-intensity. Animal welfare is the clear loser here --- their one sizable intervention gets defunded, and the -suffering accounting for non-human animals doesn't show up in drug discovery's numerator at anywhere near the same rate. The harm_robustness delta is near zero, so the trade is real on human-suffering terms but invisible to species-weighted frames. Scoring artifact risk is moderate if the operator's suffering weights undercount factory farming.

### `intv_compute` -> `intv_drug_discovery`
_Expand frontier-lab compute capacity (chips, datacenters, networking). -> Deploy frontier AI (structure prediction, candidate screening, trial simulation) inside drug discovery and therapeutic development pipelines targeting neglected infectious disease, antimicrobial resistance, and LMIC-priority therapeutics._

**Gaining:** **Global health & effective giving**, **Biomedical research**, **X-risk**, **Operator-aligned**
**Losing:** **Anthropic**, **Palantir**

Compute is the dirtiest intervention in the set (harm_robustness 0.35) --- concentration, emissions, capex-gap exposure. Moving off it to drug discovery picks up +0.45 harm robustness, the largest clean-up in the reallocation table. The friction rebinds from 'can we build the fabs and power them' to 'can we run the trials and get approval' --- slower but less socially toxic. Anthropic and Palantir lose because compute is their substrate bet; x-risk and operator-sovereignty camps gain because concentration risk drops. The -0.170 viability hit is the honest cost: drug discovery is harder to stand up than pouring more dollars into GPUs.

### `intv_alignment_research` -> `intv_drug_discovery`
_Scale funding for interpretability and alignment research. -> Deploy frontier AI (structure prediction, candidate screening, trial simulation) inside drug discovery and therapeutic development pipelines targeting neglected infectious disease, antimicrobial resistance, and LMIC-priority therapeutics._

**Gaining:** **Global health & effective giving**, **Biomedical research**
**Losing:** **X-risk**, **Anthropic**

Alignment research has almost no external friction --- it's talent-bound, not regulator- or capex-bound. Drug discovery trades that for trial-pipeline and FDA friction, which is heavier but more tractable with money. The -0.050 harm delta understates the move: alignment is a hedge against catastrophic tail risk, and shifting off it means accepting more x-risk variance for near-term DALY gains. x-risk and Anthropic coalitions lose standing because their core intervention gets treated as fungible with biomedical. Partially a scoring artifact --- long-tail catastrophic harm isn't well-priced by the harm_robustness mean.

## Reallocation blindspots

Flagged against the deterministic pair-delta --- moves the composite likely mis-prices given the camp graph:

- **`intv_alignment_research`** -> **`intv_drug_discovery`**: Alignment is tail-risk insurance; its harm_robustness of 0.85 averages across layers but doesn't capture that defunding it raises variance on civilizational-scale outcomes that drug discovery gains cannot offset in expectation.
- **`intv_compute`** -> **`intv_drug_discovery`**: Compute is enabling substrate --- drug discovery's own net_composite assumes compute exists; a literal reallocation away from compute would collapse the destination's viability, and the deterministic delta doesn't see the dependency edge.
- **`intv_alt_protein`** -> **`intv_drug_discovery`**: Drug discovery's suffering numerator is human-weighted; the delta over-sells the move against an animal-welfare frame where alt protein's numerator (billions of factory-farmed animals) is orders of magnitude larger and species-weighting isn't in the composite.
- **`intv_training`** -> **`intv_drug_discovery`**: Drug discovery's viability of 0.391 likely over-counts because it credits trial-simulation and screening gains to layers (wet-lab validation, FDA approval) that AI does not actually compress at current capability levels.
