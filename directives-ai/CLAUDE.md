# directives-ai/

Where the loop runs. Raw thoughts land here; shaped prose ships out.

## The loop

Grant drops a directive --- a raw thought, unpolished, possibly contradictory, possibly embarrassing. `claude` shapes it into a public-facing version. The shaped output is what gets committed (here or elsewhere in the repo). Raw input does not get committed.

## Inside this directory, apply the parent rules harder

All rules from `../CLAUDE.md` apply. Inside `directives-ai/`, they bind tighter because the input is explicitly raw:

- **Shape the real thought, not a surface reading of it.** A one-line directive doesn't become five paragraphs of invented scaffolding.
- **Don't resolve contradictions Grant left in place.** They may be load-bearing. Flag them; ask.
- **Don't substitute your own position for Grant's half-formed one.** A half-formed thought shaped honestly is the point. A confident take that isn't Grant's is a failure.
- **Don't smooth the voice into something blander than what came in.** Dry and precise, not corporate.
- **If the shape isn't emerging, ask.** A shaped output that misses the point is worse than no output.

## Gitignore

Raw inputs stay local. The `.gitignore` here should reflect that. Convention for what's raw vs. shaped is Grant's to set --- don't invent one unprompted.

## What doesn't live here

Already-clean writing. If Grant wrote it ready-to-ship, commit it where it belongs. This directory is for the shaping step, not a staging area for everything.
